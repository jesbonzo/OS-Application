Scheduling Algorithm

Shortest Job First (SJF)

Non-Preemptive

  • Selects job with the shortest (expected) burst time first. Shorter jobs are always executed before long jobs.
  • One major difficulty with SJF is the need to know or estimate time of each job.
  • Another difficulty is long running times may starve beacuse the CPU has a steady supply of short jobs.
  • But SJF is optimal - gives minimum WT for a given set of processes.

Example

Process AT BT
1 0 7
2 2 4
3 4 1
4 5 4

Solution:
Gantt chart:

Process
1 3 2 4

0 7 8 12 16

  • CPU = 16/16 x 100
    = 100%
  • ST = 4/16
  • TAT = End Time - Arrival Time
  • WT = Turnaround time - Burst Time
    • Process TAT WT
    1 7 - 0 = 7 7 - 7 = 0
    2 12 - 2 = 10 10 - 4 = 6
    3 8 - 4 = 4 4 - 1 = 3
    4 16 - 5 = 11 11 - 4 = 7
    • ATAT = 7+10+4+11/4
      = 32/4
      = 8
    • AWT = 0+6+3+7/4
      = 16/4
      = 4
